Studyscape

Studyscape is a vocabulary learning app that fits your learning style.

UX Case Study

2020

my role

  • UX Research
  • UX Design

I was the sole creator of this project from beginning to end.

User research, proto-persona, information architecture, wireframing & prototyping, usability testing.

00 context

This was my first ever UX project designed to understand the expereince of memorizing and understanding new concepts & terms by researching and designing an app to learn new vocabulary.

The Challenge

Design a mobile app that empowers people to learn new vocabulary based on a proto-persona developed from 3 potential users.

The Solution

A versatile, mobile study application refining memorization processes by utilizing songs, word patterns, and repetition for visual, aural, and tactile learners.

01 research

Competitor research

I conducted a competitive analysis on 3 vocabulary learning apps, Quizlet, Flashcard, and WordHolic.

User Interviews

3 potential users were interviewed. Their answers were analyzed and categorized into doing, thinking, and feeling behaviours. View interviews

02 DEFINE

Who would use this?

Since the target audience was hypothesized based on limited data, interview feedback was used to create a simpler, assumption based proto-persona named Astrid. All 3 interviewees are familiar with academia and all work in the veterinary field.

Problem statement

Astrid, a veterinary student, needs a way to study for her classes on the go because she has an active, busy lifestyle. We will know this to be true when we see that Astrid can use this mobile study app to study content how she wants, wherever she wants.

Hypothesis

I believe that by creating a mobile study application tailored to all learning styles (visual, aural, and tactile) for Astrid, she will be able to utilize her time by efficiently fitting studying into her busy lifestyle.

02 DEFINE

Task analysis

Entry point: home screen. Success criteria: user creates a fun way to learn new information.

03 DEVELOP

Wireframing, Prototyping, and Testing

Design iterations for the interface in the form of wireframes were used for initial rounds of user testing. I created a rainbow spreadsheet utilizing Jakob Nielsen's Error Scale to interpret user feedback.View Rainbow Spreadsheet A design style guide was created to ensure consistency from tested designs.

04 DELIVER

High-Fidelity Mockups

Final design iterations were adjusted based on user testing and feedback. Users desired a more "gamified" version.
