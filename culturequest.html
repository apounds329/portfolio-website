A mobile gaming application that promotes exploration and learning using tracking and mapping technology to create AR "windows to the past" at locations worldwide. CultureQuest makes the impossible possible with features like AR (augmented reality) time machine, quest abroad invites for social engagement, and cultural scavenger hunts to motivate a play-reward loop.

01 research

Competitive Analysis

I conducted two separate competitive analysis, first on mobile scavenger hunt applicaitons and then on mobile AR gaming applications. This allowed me to identify the strenghts and weaknesses of current competitiors on the market

Surveys

People ages 20-50 with an affinity for mobile gaming answered survey questions regarding preferences for game playing, exploration, and what they like most and least about current scavenger hunt and gaming applications. View Survey

Interviews

I spent some time getting to know 3 potential users, discussing thier likes & dislikes of similar games on the market, tech habits, and fantasizing about what their ideal game would be if they could create one. Interviews were conducted in person and remotely via Skype with screen record.

02 DEFINE

Understanding the User

I used the information about participants' pain points, tech habits, behavious, and needs to create user personas, stories, and journey maps. I also performed an open card sort to gather data to help establish successful hypothetical information architecture. This led to brainstorming with initial sketches and user flows.

03 DEVELOP

Wireframing, Prototyping, & Testing

Design iterations for the interface in the form of wireframes were used for initial rounds of user testing. I created a rainbow spreadsheet utilizing Jakob Nielsen's Error Scale to interpret user feedback.View Rainbow Spreadsheet A design style guide was created to ensure consistency from tested designs.

04 DELIVER

High-Fidelity Mockups

Final design iterations were adjusted based on user testing and feedback. Users desired a more "gamified" version.
