My Role

I was the sole creator of this project from beginning to end. I started with conducting research in the form of a competitive analysis, surveys, and interviews. I then created user personas, journey maps, and flows that allowed me to iterate a hypothetical concept for this mobile app in the form of low-mid- high fidelity prototypes. I then conducted user testing and re-iterated my initial designs based on data analysis from user feedback and conducted further testing. Finally, I created a high-fidelity clickable prototype for people to experience.

UX Methods

Competitive analysis, Swot profile, Business requirements, user stories, user surveys, user interviews, research analysis, affinity maps, user personas, user journey map, user flow and task analysis, site maps, low-mid-high wireframes & prototypes, card sorting, similarity matrix, 3d cluster, recruitment plan and templates, usability testing, rainbow spreadsheet, A/B testing, responsive framework, style guide.

The Challenge

Create a mobile gaming experience that can connect users to reality in a fun and relevant way. We often turn to games to escape reality, oblivious to the fascinating world all around us.

The Solution

A mobile gaming application that promotes exploration and learning using tracking and mapping technology to create AR "windows to the past" at locations worldwide.

User Research

Competitive Analysis

I conducted two separate competitive analysis, first on mobile scavenger hunt applicaitons and then on mobile AR gaming applications. This allowed me to identify the strenghts and weaknesses of current competitiors on the market

Opportunities

Surveys & Interviews

Surveys

People ages 20-50 with an affinity for mobile gaming answered survey questions regarding preferences for game playing, exploration, and what they like most and least about current scavenger hunt and gaming applications.

View Survey

Interviews

I spent some time getting to know 3 potential users, discussing thier likes & dislikes of similar games on the market, tech habits, and fantasizing about what their ideal game would be if they could create one. Interviews were conducted in person and remotely via Skype with screen record.

Affinity Mapping

Rearranging the information from interviewees into multiple categories provided different perspectives between a variety of users' needs.

Understanding the User

After conducting user interviews and surveys, I used the information about participants' pain points, tech habits, behavious, and needs to create user personas.
